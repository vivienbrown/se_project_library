Reader picks

That our readers love

  • Tales Of The Otori

    An inspiring 5 book series featuring a trilogy, prequel and sequel set in a Japanese feudal era with the added themes of fantasy, magic and mystery. The story follows Otori Takeo in his journey of self-discovery, love, and war. Otori Takeo was rescued and trained as a warrior by Otori Shigeru. Takeo falls in love with a princess while at the capital city, but must escape a group with special powers claiming him. When Takeo returns, Shigeru is gone and the country is controlled by another army. Takeo must navigate conflicting obligations and make choices that drive the story throughout the trilogy while also reconciling the conflicting spiritual beliefs and influences that he has experienced throughout his life.

    Author Gillian Rubinstein written under the pen name Lian Hearn.

  • Twirling Naked in the Street and No-one Noticed

    The author writes about her experience of living with Aspergers Syndrome. Davide-Rivera talks about events in her life, knowing she was different but was often characterized as stubborn, lazy or difficult rather than needing accommodation, and explains how being undiagnosed affected her. The end of the book discusses the change in the diagnosis of Aspergers, now re-classified under the umbrella of the Autism spectrum. As an aside, this is a good thing due to the history of Dr. Asperger who worked for Nazi Germany in identifying which Autistic people would be "useful" to the regime.

    Jeannie Davide-Rivera

  • The Mists of Avalon

    "The Mists of Avalon" is a book about the stories of King Arthur, but told from the point of view of the women. The main character is Morgaine, who is usually portrayed as a wicked sorceress, but in this book, she is shown as a determined and faithful woman who works to protect and uphold her religion against the encroachment of a rigid new faith from Rome that considers her ways a sin.

    Marion Zimmer-Bradley

  • Beginning Hydroponics

    This guide to hydroponics is an updated edition for beginners and home gardeners, providing a comprehensive introduction to soilless gardening. It covers the science, history, and mechanics of hydroponics and aquaponics, as well as different methods of hydroponic gardening like water culture and sand culture. The book also outlines the necessary materials and equipment, and provides tips for selecting plants and ensuring the right weather and lighting conditions. The guide also addresses common problems in hydroponic gardening such as pests and diseases and provides advice on how to prevent and treat them.

    Richard E. Nicholls

About the library

Libraries are essential establishments in practically every city, and Practicum's library is no exception. It first opened its doors way back in 2018, right as our humble town began bidding welcome to its very first citizens!

Since its initial opening, the library has seen nearly 342 visitors each month. That's almost 50% of our population! So far, most citizens have enjoyed reading books and making use of our ample online resources.

The library gets its name from Joan Feynman, an American astrophysicist and the first woman to be elected as an officer of the American Geophysical Union.
